Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Group Subscription?

A Group Subscription provides unlimited digital access to a select group of individuals within your organization at a discounted rate.* On-site or off-site access options are available, and your institution has the ability to manage the list of authorized users in real time. (*min. 5 users)

What is a Site License?

A Site License provides unlimited digital access to all individuals in your institution, on-site or off-site.* This is an ideal option for organizations, hotels and academic institutions that want to provide NYTimes.com access to a broad base of users while on the premises. (*Off-site access excludes hotels.)

What is the cost of each plan option?

Pricing depends on the plan option and the number of users who will have access. Price per user generally decreases with volume.

What payment options are available?

Group Subscriptions and Site Licenses are billed annually and can be paid via credit card, direct debit, check and electronic bank payment.

Is the print edition of The New York Times included with a Group Subscription or Site License?

Print copies of The New York Times are available at a discounted rate when purchased with a Group Subscription or Site License.

What is the minimum number of users necessary to purchase a Group Subscription or Site License?

A minimum of 5 users is required to purchase a Group Subscription or Site License. If you’re a student or an educator interested in a subscription for less than 5 users, please click here. All other individuals can purchase a subscription here.

My school already has a subscription. How do I access my school’s subscription?

If your school already has a subscription, you can activate your complimentary access here.

Are discounted rates available for individual students and educators?

Yes. You can find exclusive academic rates for students and educators here.

What educational resources does The New York Times offer to schools and universities?

Campus-wide subscriptions include full access to The New York Times in Education to help educators teach with The Times. Click here for more information. The Learning Network is another excellent resource that offers rich and imaginative materials for teaching and learning using New York Times content. Click here to learn more.
