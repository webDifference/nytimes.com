    Highlights

    1. Death of Jamaican Fast-Food Magnate Stuns Friends and Workers

      The 57-year-old founder, Lowell Hawthorne, started his fast-food empire in the Bronx in 1989. He committed suicide on Saturday, the police said.

      By ASHLEY SOUTHALL and SEAN PICCOLI

    2. Norman Baker, Adventurer, Dies at 89; Crossed Atlantic on Reed Raft

      He joined Thor Heyerdahl in 1970 on the Ra II, just one of the adventures that turned a Brooklyn boy into an unconventional world traveler.

      By SAM ROBERTS

      1. William Mayer, Wide-Ranging Composer, Is Dead at 91

        Mr. Mayer produced orchestral works, operas, chamber pieces, music for children and more, sometimes displaying a whimsical streak.

        By NEIL GENZLINGER

      2. Leon Cooper, Who Carried on a Battle for Tarawa, Dies at 98

        A veteran of the fight to take a Pacific atoll in World War II, he fought for the return of Marines’ remains and to restore a beach as “hallowed ground.”

        By RICHARD SANDOMIR

    1. Vincent Scully, 97, Influential Architecture Historian, Dies

      “I think he probably did more than anyone else over the last 60 years to affect not just architecture but architecture culture as well,” one disciple said.

      By RICHARD B. WOODWARD

    2. Mitch Margo, an Original Member of the Tokens, Dies at 70

      Mr. Margo helped write a number of songs for the group (although not its biggest hit, “The Lion Sleeps Tonight”). he later wrote music for television.

      By MAYA SALAM

    3. Jim Nabors, 87, TV’s Gomer Pyle, Is Dead

      Mr. Nabors found fame as an amiable bumpkin while pursuing as a second career as a singer with a booming baritone voice.

      By RICHARD SEVERO

    4. Jack Doroshow, 78, Drag Pageant Impresario, Dies

      Mr. Doroshow organized pageants in the 1960s, before the gay- and transgender-rights movements had gotten rolling. He hosted them as Flawless Sabrina.

      By NEIL GENZLINGER

    5. Notable Deaths 2017

      Remembering Mary Tyler Moore, John Hurt, William Peter Blatty and others who died this year.

    Latest Articles

    1. Jerry A. Fodor, Philosopher Who Plumbed the Mind’s Depths, Dies at 82

      Dr. Fodor brought the workings of 20th-century computer technology to bear on ancient questions about the structure of human cognition.

      By MARGALIT FOX
      Nov. 30, 2017

    2. Shadia, Egyptian Actress and Singer, Is Dead

      Known for her silky and playful voice, she had roles in more than 100 movies and was part of what is considered a “golden” age of Egyptian film.

      By THE ASSOCIATED PRESS
      Nov. 30, 2017

    3. W. Marvin Watson, Johnson’s Unofficial Chief of Staff, Dies at 93

      He spurned several job offers from Lyndon Johnson before finally accepting the role of White House gatekeeper, though without the chief of staff title.

      By DOUGLAS MARTIN
      Nov. 29, 2017

    4. Armando Hart, Who Revolutionized Cuban Schools, Dies at 87

      An early confidant of Fidel Castro, he was credited with greatly improving the nation’s literacy rate as the regime’s first minister of education.

      By SAM ROBERTS
      Nov. 29, 2017

    5. Ken Shapiro, Whose ‘Groove Tube’ Satirized TV, Dies at 75

      Mr. Shapiro’s film, with a cast that included Chevy Chase, anticipated “Saturday Night Live” by a year. But he stopped making movies before he turned 40.

      By RICHARD SANDOMIR
      Nov. 29, 2017

    6. Snapper Jones, N.B.A. Broadcaster, Dies at 75

      Jones, an A.B.A. All-Star, was a longtime broadcaster for the Portland Trail Blazers and reached a national audience through ESPN, NBC and other networks.

      By THE ASSOCIATED PRESS
      Nov. 29, 2017

    7. S.I. Newhouse Jr., Remembered: ‘Eager, Never Jaded’

      At a memorial for Condé Nast’s former chairman, tearful speakers and raucous anecdotes.

      By JACOB BERNSTEIN
      Nov. 29, 2017

    8. Harry Pregerson, Judge Guided by Conscience, Dies at 94

      Named by Presidents Johnson and Carter, he served on the Ninth Circuit bench in California as a liberal whose priority was equal protection.

      By SAM ROBERTS
      Nov. 29, 2017

    9. Carol Neblett, Soprano at the Met and City Opera, Dies at 71

      Ms. Neblett performed numerous roles over many years but may have drawn the most attention for one in New Orleans in which she shed her clothes.

      By NEIL GENZLINGER
      Nov. 28, 2017

    10. Rabbi Neil Gillman, Theologian of Conservative Judaism, Dies at 84

      Rabbi Gillman sought new ways to talk about God, death and the afterlife. He also championed the ordination of women and gays.

      By JOSEPH BERGER
      Nov. 28, 2017

    2. Vera Shlakman, Professor Fired During Red Scare, Dies at 108

      She had taught economics at Queens College and written a groundbreaking book when she refused to say if she was a Communist. An apology came years later.

      By SAM ROBERTS
      Nov. 28, 2017

    3. Wayne Cochran, Singer With High Energy and Big Hair, Dies at 78

      Mr. Cochran wrote a classic song of doomed love, “Last Kiss,” then changed styles to become “the White Knight of Soul.”

      By NEIL GENZLINGER
      Nov. 28, 2017

    4. Rance Howard, Actor and the Father of Ron Howard, Dies at 89

      For more than 60 years, Mr. Howard appeared frequently on television and in films, including many directed by his son.

      By THE ASSOCIATED PRESS
      Nov. 26, 2017

    5. Pete Moore, an Original Miracle and Co-Writer of Hits, Dies at 79

      As an integral member of the group led by his childhood friend Smokey Robinson, Mr. Moore played an important role in Motown Records’ origin story.

      By RICHARD SANDOMIR
      Nov. 26, 2017

    6. Eric Salzman, Composer Who Championed Avant-Garde, Dies at 84

      Mr. Salzman, who was also a music critic and book author, was a founder of the American Music Theater Festival in Philadelphia.

      By NEIL GENZLINGER
      Nov. 24, 2017

    7. Mary Adelman, 89, Fixer of Broken Typewriters, Is Dead

      For decades, her shop on the Upper West Side of Manhattan was a fixture in the lives of writers desperate to keep the words flowing.

      By JAMES BARRON
      Nov. 24, 2017

    8. C.E. Meyer Jr., 89, Dies; as T.W.A. Chief, He Fought a Takeover

      Mr. Meyer tried unsuccessfully to thwart the corporate raider Carl Icahn’s bid to take over the troubled airline in 1985.

      By KATIE BENNER
      Nov. 24, 2017

    9. Maurice D. Hinchey, Congressman and Environmental Advocate, Dies at 79

      Mr. Hinchey, a Democrat from New York who served 10 terms in Congress, built a reputation as a champion of the environment and blue-collar workers.

      By MATT STEVENS
      Nov. 23, 2017

    10. Naim Suleymanoglu, 50, Dies; Weight Lifting’s ‘Pocket Hercules’

      At 4-foot-10, he won three consecutive Olympic gold medals in weight lifting, emerging as a national hero in Turkey.

      By RICHARD SANDOMIR
      Nov. 22, 2017

    11. Jon Hendricks, 96, Who Brought a New Dimension to Jazz Singing, Dies

      His work with the vocal trio Lambert, Hendricks & Ross established him as a master of adding words to jazz instrumentals.

      By PETER KEEPNEWS
      Nov. 22, 2017
